# Emergency / EID Homework Notes

This folder contains course materials and drafted answers for **CMED 6104 (Emerging Infectious Diseases)**, focused here on **Session 3 (“From SARS to COVID-19”)**.

## Summary (可直接貼到作業前言)

### 中文（總結）

本作業以「SARS 到 COVID‑19」為主軸，回答五題：在 2019 年 12 月武漢早期不明肺炎情境下，應優先釐清是否存在持續人傳人與無症狀/前症狀傳播、估計 \(R_0\) 與世代間隔，並以更寬鬆的病例定義、主動監測、快速採樣與資料共享來降低不確定性；控制策略上主張分層措施（隔離、檢測追蹤、及早啟動 NPI）並把「溢出風險」的上游驅動因素（人畜接觸、野生動物交易、土地利用變遷、農場生物安全）納入 One Health 的預防框架；在國際層面則以 IHR 的通報與協作為核心並討論其改革方向。最後以「病例/死亡以外的衝擊指標」（morbidity、mortality、disability、成本、YPLL、DALY）建立比較架構，解釋 2020–2022 年香港、美國與紐西蘭在政策強度、監測能力、疫苗與變異株時序等因素下呈現的差異。

### English (abstract)

This submission answers five Session 3 questions spanning early outbreak decision-making, national/international interventions, One Health drivers, transmission control, and impact measurement. It argues that in December 2019 Wuhan, uncertainty should accelerate action: rapidly test for sustained human-to-human and silent transmission, estimate \(R_0\) and the generation interval, broaden case definitions, and strengthen active case finding, sampling, and data sharing. Control requires layered measures (isolation capacity, early NPIs, surveillance/trace/test), while upstream spillover drivers (human–animal contact, wildlife trade, land-use change, farm biosecurity) must be integrated into preparedness. It also discusses IHR notification/cooperation and post‑COVID reforms. Finally, it proposes a basket of burden metrics beyond cases/deaths (morbidity, mortality, disability, costs, YPLL, DALYs) to interpret differences across Hong Kong, the USA, and New Zealand from 2020–2022 under shifting policies, vaccination, waning, and variant waves.
